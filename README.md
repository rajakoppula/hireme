## The short version

For fifteen years I've been shipping things on the internet. Some became six-figure machines. A few crashed beautifully. Every single one taught me how digital products are actually **found, grown, and monetized** — not in theory, but in the wild.

It started back in 2011; by 2013 I had pushed a wallpaper blog past **100K visits a month** with nothing but curiosity and SEO. That was the hook. Since then I've built torrent metasearch engines, local discovery platforms, affiliate brands, viral content sites, and lead-gen funnels — learning the full stack of growth by doing it, breaking it, and rebuilding it better.

Then the ground shifted. Classic SEO is crowded and commoditized now. The real frontier is **being visible to machines** — getting your brand cited by LLMs, surfaced in AI answers, and ranked inside answer engines. That's **GEO and AEO**, and it's the widest-open opportunity I've seen in a decade. Most people see the change as a threat. I see it as a land grab.

And I no longer stop at strategy. With modern AI tooling I now **design, build, and deploy real products** — WordPress sites, custom web apps, deployable tools — taking an idea from a sketch to a live, working URL. Strategist on the whiteboard, builder in the codebase. Same person.

> I'm not here to sell consulting hours. I'm here to build systems that compound.
